# Motorcycle Insurance Policy Terms and Conditions

## 1. Overview and Purpose

This document outlines the comprehensive terms and conditions governing the Motorcycle Insurance Policy issued by [Insurance Company Name] (hereinafter referred to as "the Company"). Its purpose is to clearly define the contractual agreement between the policyholder and the Company, detailing coverage, responsibilities, premiums, claims procedures, and exclusions to ensure mutual understanding and compliance. This policy is designed to protect motorcycle owners against financial loss arising from theft, damage, or liability to third parties.

## 2. Scope of Coverage

This policy provides coverage for the Insured Motorcycle(s) specified in the Policy Schedule and the Named Insured(s) under the conditions described herein. Coverage applies within the geographical limits of [Specify Geographic Region, e.g., United States and Canada] and is active only for the policy period stated in the Policy Schedule, provided all premiums are paid in full and on time.

### 2.1 Included Coverages

*   **Collision Coverage:** Covers damage to your motorcycle resulting from a collision with another vehicle or object, regardless of fault.
*   **Comprehensive Coverage:** Covers non-collision damage to your motorcycle, such as theft, vandalism, fire, natural disasters, and impact with an animal.
*   **Bodily Injury Liability:** Covers financial responsibility for injuries you cause to others in an accident involving your motorcycle.
*   **Property Damage Liability:** Covers financial responsibility for damage you cause to another person's property in an accident involving your motorcycle.
*   **Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UM/UIM) Coverage:** Covers your medical expenses and property damage if you are hit by an uninsured or underinsured driver.
*   **Medical Payments Coverage:** Covers reasonable and necessary medical expenses for you and your passengers resulting from a motorcycle accident, regardless of fault.
*   **Custom Parts & Equipment Coverage:** Provides coverage for aftermarket parts, accessories, and customizations permanently attached to the motorcycle. Standard limit is $3,000, but can be increased by endorsement.
*   **Roadside Assistance:** Covers towing and labor costs if your motorcycle becomes disabled.

### 2.2 Excluded Coverages

This policy does not cover:
*   Damage due to wear and tear, mechanical breakdown, or inherent vice.
*   Any motorcycle used for racing, stunt riding, or competitive events.
*   Damage intentionally caused by the Insured.
*   Loss or damage occurring while the motorcycle is being operated by an unauthorized driver (not listed on the policy or without a valid license).
*   Seizure or destruction by government or civil authorities.
*   Nuclear hazard or war risks.
*   Loss of income or reduction in value of the motorcycle unless explicitly covered.

## 3. Definitions

*   **Insured Motorcycle:** The specific motorcycle(s) described in the declarations page of this policy.
*   **Named Insured:** The individual(s) or entity listed on the policy declarations page as the primary policyholder.
*   **Policy Period:** The duration for which the policy is in effect, as specified in the Policy Schedule.
*   **Deductible:** The amount of money the policyholder must pay out-of-pocket before the insurance coverage begins to pay for a covered loss.
*   **Premium:** The payment required from the policyholder to the Company for active coverage.
*   **Collision:** Impact of an automobile or trailer with another object or upset of the automobile or trailer.
*   **Comprehensive:** Any direct and accidental loss to the Insured Motorcycle except loss caused by collision or upset. This includes but is not limited to impact with birds or animals, fire, theft, vandalism, falling objects, explosion, earthquake, windstorm, hail, water, or flood.
*   **Custom Parts & Equipment:** Any permanent alteration, addition, or enhancement to the motorcycle's original manufacturer specifications. Includes chrome, special paint, modifications to engine, frame, exhaust, and electronic systems.
*   **Total Loss:** When the cost to repair the damaged motorcycle exceeds a predetermined percentage (typically 75-80%) of its Actual Cash Value (ACV) immediately prior to the loss event.
*   **Actual Cash Value (ACV):** The fair market value of the Insured Motorcycle at the time of loss, taking into account depreciation, wear, and tear.

## 4. Policyholder Responsibilities

The policyholder agrees to:
*   Pay premiums on or before their due dates.
*   Notify the Company promptly of any changes that may affect the policy, such as changes in address, primary operator, modifications to the motorcycle, or conviction of driving offenses.
*   Cooperate fully in the event of a claim, providing accurate and timely information.
*   Maintain the Insured Motorcycle in a safe and roadworthy condition.
*   Immediately notify law enforcement in case of theft or vandalism.
*   Provide all required documentation as requested by the Company for underwriting or claim processing.

## 5. Premium Calculation and Payment

### 5.1 Premium Factors

Premiums are determined based on several factors, including but not limited to:
*   **Motorcycle Type:** Make, model, year, engine size, and value.
*   **Operator History:** Driving record of all listed drivers, including accidents, violations, and license status.
*   **Geographic Location:** Risk associated with the primary garaging address.
*   **Coverage Limits & Deductibles:** Higher limits and lower deductibles generally result in higher premiums.
*   **Usage:** Estimated annual mileage and primary use (e.g., pleasure, commuting).
*   **Security Devices:** Presence of anti-theft devices (e.g., GPS trackers, alarms) may qualify for discounts.
*   **Customizations:** Extensive modifications or high-value custom parts may increase premiums.

### 5.2 Payment Schedule

Premiums are due as specified in the Policy Schedule. Payment options typically include:
*   Annual single payment.
*   Semi-annual installments.
*   Quarterly installments.
*   Monthly installments (may incur an additional service charge).
Failure to pay premiums by the due date may result in policy lapse or cancellation as per section 8.1.

## 6. Claims Procedures

### 6.1 Reporting a Claim

In the event of a loss or accident, the policyholder must:
1.  **Notify the Company Immediately:** Report the claim online at [Website URL], via the mobile app, or by calling [Claims Phone Number] within 72 hours of the incident, or as soon as reasonably possible.
2.  **Ensure Safety:** Take reasonable steps to prevent further damage to the motorcycle.
3.  **Contact Law Enforcement:** For theft, vandalism, or accidents involving other vehicles, immediately notify the local police.
4.  **Provide Information:** Be prepared to provide the policy number, date, time, and location of the incident, a description of what occurred, and contact information for any other parties involved and witnesses.
5.  **Submit Documentation:** Provide any requested documentation, such as police reports, photos of damage, repair estimates, and proof of ownership.

### 6.2 Claim Processing and Settlement

*   **Assessment:** A claims adjuster will be assigned to assess the damage to the Insured Motorcycle. This may involve physical inspection, review of repair estimates, and determination of ACV for total loss scenarios.
*   **Repair or Replacement:** For partial losses, the Company will authorize repairs to an approved repair facility. For total losses, the Company will pay the Actual Cash Value of the motorcycle at the time of loss, less any applicable deductible.
*   **Deductibles:** The applicable deductible (as specified in the Policy Schedule) will be subtracted from the total settlement amount for collision and comprehensive claims.
*   **Custom Parts & Equipment Claims:** For custom parts, photographic evidence or receipts may be required to validate the value. Coverage is subject to the stated limits for Custom Parts & Equipment.
*   **Liability Claims:** The Company will investigate liability claims, negotiate with third parties, and pay covered damages up to the policy limits.

## 7. Endorsements and Modifications

Any alteration or addition to the policy terms, such as increased coverage limits, change of motorcycle, or addition of a new driver, will require a formal endorsement issued by the Company. Endorsements may result in adjustments to the premium. All requests for modifications must be submitted in writing or through approved digital channels.

## 8. Policy Cancellation and Non-Renewal

### 8.1 Cancellation by Policyholder

The policyholder may cancel this policy at any time by providing written notice to the Company. The cancellation will be effective upon receipt of the notice or a later date specified by the policyholder. A prorated refund of unearned premium will be issued, subject to any short-rate penalties if applicable per state regulations.

### 8.2 Cancellation by Company

The Company may cancel this policy for reasons including, but not limited to:
*   **Non-payment of Premium:** If premium is not received by the due date. A 10-day notice will be provided before cancellation.
*   **Material Misrepresentation:** If the policyholder provided false or misleading information during the application process.
*   **Increase in Hazard:** If the risk associated with the motorcycle or policyholder significantly increases rendering the policy unsuitable (e.g., conviction of serious driving offenses).
*   **Fraudulent Claim:** If a claim submitted by the policyholder is found to be fraudulent.
The Company will provide written notice of cancellation as required by state law, typically 20-30 days prior to the effective cancellation date.

### 8.3 Non-Renewal by Company

The Company reserves the right not to renew this policy at the end of the policy period. Written notice of non-renewal will be provided to the policyholder at least 30 to 60 days (depending on state regulations) before the policy's expiration date.

## 9. General Provisions

*   **Subrogation:** If the Company pays a claim, it acquires the right to pursue recovery from any party responsible for the loss.
*   **Assignment:** This policy cannot be assigned to another party without the Company's written consent.
*   **Fraud:** Any attempt to defraud the Company, including false statements or omissions in the application or claim process, will result in policy voidance and rejection of claims.
*   **Governing Law:** This policy shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the state where the policy is issued.
