# Commercial Auto Policy Terms

## 1. Overview and Purpose

This document outlines the complete contractual terms and conditions for commercial auto insurance policies issued by [Your Company Name], hereafter referred to as "the Insurer." Its purpose is to define the rights and obligations of both the Policyholder and the Insurer, detailing coverage, exclusions, claims procedures, and premium calculations for vehicles used in business operations, including fleet coverage. This policy is designed to protect businesses against financial losses arising from accidents involving their owned, leased, or hired vehicles.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all commercial auto insurance contracts, primary and excess, issued for vehicles registered and operated within the United States and its territories. It covers vehicles listed on the policy declarations page, as well as newly acquired vehicles, under specific conditions outlined herein. This document supersedes any previous general terms and conditions relating to commercial auto insurance specifically for policies effective on or after the `effective_date`.

### 2.1 Included Coverage Types
*   Bodily Injury Liability
*   Property Damage Liability
*   Collision Coverage
*   Comprehensive Coverage
*   Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist Coverage
*   Medical Payments/Personal Injury Protection (PIP)
*   Hired Auto Coverage
*   Non-Owned Auto Coverage
*   Trailer Interchange Coverage

### 2.2 Exclusions
This policy does not cover:
*   Vehicles used for personal, non-business purposes unless explicitly endorsed.
*   Vehicles not listed on the policy declarations, except as per Section 4.5 (Newly Acquired Vehicles).
*   Intentional acts causing damage or injury.
*   Losses due to war, rebellion, or nuclear hazard.
*   Vehicles rented to others or used for ride-sharing services without specific endorsement.
*   Damage due to wear and tear, freezing, mechanical or electrical breakdown.
*   Losses occurring outside the policy's defined territory (USA and its territories).

## 3. Definitions

*   **Policyholder:** The individual or entity named on the declarations page as the insured.
*   **Insurer:** [Your Company Name], the entity providing the insurance coverage.
*   **Insured Vehicle:** Any vehicle specifically described in the policy declarations, or any "newly acquired auto" as defined herein.
*   **Accident:** A sudden, unforeseen, and unintended event that results in bodily injury or property damage.
*   **Bodily Injury:** Physical harm, sickness, or disease sustained by a person, including death resulting from any of these.
*   **Property Damage:** Physical injury to, destruction of, or loss of use of tangible property.
*   **Deductible:** The amount of loss in an insured event that the Policyholder must pay before the Insurer pays any amount.
*   **Fleet:** A group of five (5) or more distinct vehicles under common ownership, utilized for commercial purposes, and insured under a single policy.
*   **Endorsement:** A written amendment to the policy that changes its terms or conditions.
*   **Financial Responsibility Law:** Any law that requires evidence of financial responsibility to be maintained by or on behalf of owners or operators of motor vehicles.
*   **Loss:** Direct and accidental loss or damage.

## 4. Main Content: Policy Provisions and Coverage Details

### 4.1 Eligibility Criteria for Coverage
To be eligible for coverage, the Policyholder must:
*   Be a registered business entity or individual operating a business.
*   Own, lease, or hire vehicles primarily for commercial purposes.
*   Maintain a valid driver's license for all operators of insured vehicles.
*   Provide accurate and complete information in the insurance application.
*   Comply with all state and federal regulations regarding vehicle operation and insurance.

### 4.2 Liability Coverage
*   **Bodily Injury Liability:** The Insurer will pay sums the Insured is legally obligated to pay as damages because of bodily injury caused by an accident and resulting from the ownership, maintenance, or use of a covered auto. The limit of liability is specified on the declarations page.
*   **Property Damage Liability:** The Insurer will pay sums the Insured is legally obligated to pay as damages because of property damage caused by an accident and resulting from the ownership, maintenance, or use of a covered auto. The limit of liability is specified on the declarations page.

### 4.3 Physical Damage Coverage
*   **Collision Coverage:** Covers loss to a covered auto caused by its upset or impact with another vehicle or object. A deductible applies as shown on the declarations page.
*   **Comprehensive Coverage:** Covers loss to a covered auto caused by any event other than its upset or impact with another vehicle or object, such as fire, theft, vandalism, falling objects, explosion, earthquake, windstorm, hail, and flood. A deductible applies as shown on the declarations page.
*   **Glass Breakage:** No deductible applies to glass breakage under Comprehensive Coverage unless specifically stated otherwise on the declarations page.

### 4.4 Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UM/UIM) Coverage
Covers bodily injury and property damage to an Insured due to an accident caused by a driver who is uninsured or underinsured, up to the limits specified on the declarations page. This coverage aligns with state statutory requirements and elections made by the Policyholder.

### 4.5 Newly Acquired Vehicles
*   **Eligibility:** A newly acquired auto is covered if it replaces a vehicle shown on the declarations page or if the Policyholder acquires additional vehicles.
*   **Reporting Period:** The Policyholder must notify the Insurer of the acquisition within thirty (30) days of the date of purchase.
*   **Coverage Extent:** The newly acquired auto will have the broadest coverage applicable to any vehicle already listed on the policy, unless specific coverage is requested and endorsed.

### 4.6 Premium Calculation and Payment Terms
*   **Factors:** Premiums are calculated based on vehicle type, usage, garaging location, driver histories, coverage limits, deductibles, and any applicable endorsements.
*   **Payment Schedule:** Premiums are due on the dates specified in the payment plan agreement.
*   **Non-Payment:** Failure to pay premiums by the due date may result in policy cancellation after a thirty (30) day notice period. Reinstatement may be subject to a lapse in coverage and additional fees.

## 5. Requirements and Obligations

### 5.1 Policyholder Obligations
*   Provide accurate and timely information.
*   Pay premiums as due.
*   Maintain covered autos in good working condition.
*   Report accidents and losses promptly as per Section 6.
*   Cooperate with the Insurer in the investigation, settlement, or defense of any claim or suit.
*   Notify the Insurer of any changes in business operations, vehicle usage, or driver lists within thirty (30) days.

### 5.2 Insurer Obligations
*   Process claims fairly and efficiently.
*   Provide coverage as outlined in the policy.
*   Defend and settle covered claims.
*   Provide timely communication regarding policy changes and renewals.

## 6. Claims Procedures

### 6.1 Reporting a Claim
*   **Immediate Notification:** The Policyholder must notify the Insurer as soon as practicable after an accident or loss, ideally within 24-48 hours.
*   **Information Required:**
    *   Date, time, and location of the accident.
    *   Description of the incident.
    *   Names and contact information of all involved parties (drivers, passengers, witnesses).
    *   Vehicle information for all vehicles involved (make, model, license plate, VIN).
    *   Police report number, if applicable.
    *   Photographs of damage and scene (if available).

### 6.2 Investigation and Adjustment
*   The Insurer will assign a claims adjuster to investigate the loss.
*   The Policyholder must cooperate fully, providing access to vehicles, records, and relevant personnel.
*   The Insurer reserves the right to inspect damaged vehicles prior to repair authorization.

### 6.3 Settlement and Payment
*   Once liability and damages are determined, the Insurer will attempt to negotiate a settlement.
*   Payment will be made to the appropriate party (Policyholder, repair shop, third party claimant) less any applicable deductible.
*   For total losses, settlement will be based on the actual cash value (ACV) of the vehicle at the time of loss, determined by recognized valuation methods (e.g., NADA, Kelley Blue Book, market analysis).

## 7. Exceptions

### 7.1 Emergency Repairs
In cases of emergency where a covered auto is undrivable and immediate repairs are necessary to prevent further damage, the Policyholder may authorize repairs up to $1,000 without prior Insurer approval. All such repairs must be documented with receipts and reported to the Insurer within 48 hours.

### 7.2 Non-Standard Vehicles
Vehicles operating outside typical commercial uses (e.g., exotic vehicles, heavily modified vehicles, vehicles used for racing or demolition) require specific underwriting review and may be subject to unique endorsements, higher premiums, or exclusion from coverage.

## 9. Decision Logic: Policy Cancellation

### 9.1 Insurer-Initiated Cancellation
The Insurer may cancel this policy for the following reasons:
*   **Non-payment of Premium:** If any premium installment is not paid by its due date, the Insurer will send a notice of cancellation providing a minimum of thirty (30) days' notice. Cancellation effective on the date specified in the notice.
*   **Material Misrepresentation:** If the Insurer discovers material misrepresentation of facts in the application or during the policy term, a notice of cancellation will be issued with thirty (30) days' notice.
*   **Increased Hazard:** If there is a substantial change in the risk originally insured that is not disclosed or mitigated, the Insurer may cancel with thirty (30) days' notice.
*   **Fraudulent Claim:** Discovery of any fraudulent claim will result in immediate cancellation, effective from the date of the fraudulent act, with appropriate notice provided.
*   **Violation of Policy Terms:** Persistent or severe violation of other policy terms and conditions, after reasonable warning, may lead to cancellation with thirty (30) days' notice.

### 9.2 Policyholder-Initiated Cancellation
The Policyholder may cancel this policy at any time by providing written notice to the Insurer. Cancellation will be effective on the date the notice is received by the Insurer, or a later date if specified in the notice. Premiums for the unexpired policy term will be refunded on a pro-rata basis, less any applicable cancellation fees.

## 10. Evidence and Documentation

The following documents may be required for policy issuance, renewal, or claims processing:
*   Commercial auto insurance application (ACORD forms, supplemental applications).
*   Certificate of incorporation or business registration documents.
*   Vehicle registration copies (for all insured vehicles).
*   Driver lists with MVR (Motor Vehicle Record) reports for each driver.
*   Lease agreements for leased vehicles.
*   Proof of prior insurance.
*   Accident reports (police reports, incident reports).
*   Repair estimates and invoices.
*   Medical records for bodily injury claims.
*   Photos or videos of accidents/damages.

## 11. Timelines

*   **Claim Reporting:** Within 24-48 hours of loss (see Section 6.1).
*   **Newly Acquired Vehicle Notification:** Within thirty (30) days of purchase (see Section 4.5).
*   **Premium Payment Grace Period:** Varies by state, typically 10-30 days before cancellation notice is issued.
*   **Policy Cancellation Notice Period (Insurer):** Minimum of thirty (30) days for non-payment or other reasons.
*   **Claim Resolution:** Varies by complexity, but the Insurer aims to resolve undisputed claims within 30-90 days from full documentation submission.
