# General Insurance Definitions

## Overview/Purpose
This document provides a comprehensive glossary of standard terms, phrases, and concepts commonly used across all general insurance policy contracts issued by [Your Company Name]. The purpose of these definitions is to ensure clarity, consistency, and a shared understanding of policy language for all parties involved: policyholders, claimants, underwriting, and claims management teams. These definitions are binding for the interpretation of all policy wording unless expressly stated otherwise within a specific policy schedule or endorsement.

## Scope
These definitions apply to all general insurance products offered by [Your Company Name], including but not limited to: Property Insurance, Motor Insurance, Liability Insurance, Marine Insurance, Travel Insurance, and Accident & Health Insurance. This document does not supersede any specific definitions provided in individual policy wordings that might be tailored to a particular product or risk, but rather serves as a foundational reference.

## Definitions

### A
*   **Accident:** A sudden, unforeseen, and unintended event that results in bodily injury or property damage, occurring at an identifiable time and place.
*   **Act of God:** An irresistible superhuman cause, such as an earthquake or a flood, that is independent of human intervention and cannot be prevented by reasonable foresight or care.
*   **Actual Cash Value (ACV):** The cost to replace insured property with new property of like kind and quality, less depreciation. Depreciation is calculated based on factors such as age, condition, and obsolescence.
*   **Additional Insured:** A person or entity, other than the named insured, who is protected under the policy. This status typically requires an endorsement to the policy.
*   **Adjuster:** An individual who investigates claims and recommends settlement amounts based on the policy contract and an assessment of damages. Can be an employee of the insurer (staff adjuster) or an independent contractor.
*   **Aggregate Limit:** The maximum amount an insurer will pay for all covered losses during a policy period, regardless of the number of claims or occurrences.

### B
*   **Beneficiary:** The person or entity designated by the policyholder to receive the proceeds of a policy, typically in life or specific personal accident policies.
*   **Bodily Injury:** Physical injury, sickness, disease, or death sustained by a person, including mental anguish or suffering that results from the physical injury.
*   **Broker:** An independent agent who represents the insured and can arrange insurance with various insurers.

### C
*   **Cancellation:** The termination of an insurance policy by the insurer or the insured before its natural expiration date.
*   **Claim:** A formal request by a policyholder to the insurance company for payment for a loss covered by the policy.
*   **Claimant:** The person or entity making a claim.
*   **Condition:** A provision in an insurance policy that qualifies or places limitations on the insurer's promise to pay or perform. Breach of a condition may void the policy or a claim.
*   **Coverage:** The protection provided by an insurance policy.
*   **Cyber Incident:** Any unauthorized access, disruption, or destruction of an information system or network, or its data, including but not limited to malicious software attacks, denial of service attacks, or data breaches.

### D
*   **Deductible:** The amount of loss or expense that the insured is responsible for before the insurer will pay for a covered loss.
*   **Depreciation:** The decrease in value of property over time due to wear and tear, age, or obsolescence.
*   **Duty of Disclosure:** The insured's obligation to provide all material facts to the insurer during the application process. Failure to do so may result in the policy being voided.

### E
*   **Effective Date:** The date on which an insurance policy or coverage officially begins.
*   **Endorsement:** A written amendment added to an insurance policy that changes, adds, or deletes provisions of the policy.
*   **Exclusion:** A provision in an insurance policy that specifies situations or losses for which the policy will not provide coverage.

### F
*   **Fraud:** Deliberate misrepresentation or concealment by the policyholder or claimant to induce the insurer to pay a claim or issue a policy that it would not otherwise have.
*   **Force Majeure:** (See "Act of God")

### I
*   **Inception Date:** The date on which the insurance coverage begins. (See "Effective Date")
*   **Indemnity:** The principle by which insurance policies compensate policyholders for their actual losses, aiming to restore them to the financial position they were in before the loss, without profit.
*   **Insured:** The person, persons, or entity protected by the insurance policy.
*   **Insurer:** The insurance company providing the financial protection against specified losses.

### L
*   **Liability:** Legal responsibility for damage or injury caused to another person or property.
*   **Limit of Liability:** The maximum amount the insurer will pay for a covered loss, as stated in the policy.
*   **Loss:** The amount of damage or financial detriment caused by an insured peril, or the event itself that triggers the need for a claim.

### M
*   **Material Fact:** Any information that would influence an insurer's decision whether to accept a risk, and if so, on what terms and premium. Both positive and negative facts are considered material.
*   **Mitigation of Loss:** The insured's duty to take all reasonable steps to prevent or reduce further damage after a loss has occurred.

### N
*   **Named Insured:** The individual or entity specifically named in the policy declarations as the insured.

### O
*   **Occurrence:** An accident, including continuous or repeated exposure to substantially the same general harmful conditions, which results in bodily injury or property damage.

### P
*   **Peril:** The cause of a loss, such as fire, flood, theft, or an accident.
*   **Policy:** The written contract of insurance between the insurer and the insured.
*   **Policyholder:** The person or entity who owns the insurance policy.
*   **Premium:** The amount of money paid by the policyholder to the insurer for insurance coverage.
*   **Property Damage:** Physical injury to, destruction of, or loss of use of tangible property.

### R
*   **Renewal:** The continuation of an insurance policy beyond its initial term.
*   **Replacement Cost (RC):** The cost to repair or replace damaged property with new property of like kind and quality, without deduction for depreciation.
*   **Risk:** The chance of a loss occurring, or the subject matter of an insurance policy.

### S
*   **Salvage:** Damaged property that the insurer takes ownership of after paying out a claim, typically to recover some of the loss by selling it.
*   **Schedule:** The part of the policy that provides specific details about the insured, the sums insured, the premium, and the period of insurance.
*   **Subrogation:** The legal right of an insurer to pursue a third party that caused an insurance loss to the insured. The insurer steps into the shoes of the insured to recover the amount it paid on the claim.
*   **Sum Insured:** The maximum amount of money the insurer will pay in the event of a covered loss, as specified in the policy schedule.

### T
*   **Third Party:** A person or entity not a direct party to the insurance contract (not the insurer or the insured) but who may be affected by the actions of the insured and have a claim.

### U
*   **Underwriter:** An insurance professional who evaluates and assesses potential risks to determine the terms, conditions, and premium for insurance coverage.
*   **Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist Coverage:** Coverage that protects the insured against losses caused by drivers who do not have adequate liability insurance.

### V
*   **Void Policy:** An insurance policy that is considered to have never been legally in effect, often due to fraud or material misrepresentation at the time of application.

## Exceptions
*   Specific product lines or highly specialized policies may include additional or modified definitions that explicitly override or supplement those provided herein. In such cases, the definitions within the specific policy wording shall take precedence.
*   These definitions do not replace legal or regulatory definitions where such are mandated by law.
