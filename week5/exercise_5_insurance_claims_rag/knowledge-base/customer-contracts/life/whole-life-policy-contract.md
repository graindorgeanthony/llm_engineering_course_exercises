# Whole Life Insurance Policy Contract

## 1. Overview

This Whole Life Insurance Policy Contract ("Policy" or "Contract") is a legally binding agreement between the Policyholder and [Insurance Company Name] (referred to herein as "Company," "We," "Us," or "Our"). This Policy provides a guaranteed death benefit to the designated Beneficiary(ies) upon the death of the Insured, along with a cash value component that accumulates over time on a tax-deferred basis. It is designed to offer lifelong coverage with fixed premiums.

## 2. Scope

This Policy covers the life of the individual named as the Insured on the Policy Declarations page. It specifies the terms, conditions, benefits, and obligations of both the Policyholder and the Company regarding the Whole Life insurance coverage. This Contract, including the Policy Declarations, application, and any attached riders or endorsements, constitutes the entire agreement.

### 2.1 Included Coverage

*   Guaranteed death benefit payable to Beneficiary(ies).
*   Guaranteed cash value accumulation.
*   Fixed premium payments for the life of the Insured (or until premiums are paid up, if applicable).
*   Policy loan availability.
*   Non-forfeiture options.

### 2.2 Excluded Coverage

This Policy does not cover:
*   Death resulting directly or indirectly from suicide within two (2) years from the Issue Date of this Policy or the effective date of any increase in coverage if such increase is subject to a new suicide exclusion period. In such cases, the Company's liability will be limited to a refund of premiums paid, less any outstanding policy loans.
*   Losses caused by war or act of war, as defined in Section 7.5.
*   Fraudulent misrepresentation in the application that materially affects the Company's acceptance of the risk.

## 3. Definitions

*   **Anniversary Date:** The same day and month as the Issue Date in each subsequent calendar year.
*   **Beneficiary(ies):** The person(s) or entity designated by the Policyholder to receive the Death Benefit.
*   **Cash Value:** The amount of money accumulated within the Policy, which is guaranteed to grow at a specified rate and can be accessed by the Policyholder.
*   **Death Benefit:** The sum of money payable to the Beneficiary(ies) upon the death of the Insured, as stated in the Policy Declarations.
*   **Effective Date:** The date coverage under this Policy officially begins.
*   **Insured:** The person whose life is covered by this Policy.
*   **Issue Date:** The date the Policy is issued by the Company.
*   **Lapse:** The termination of the Policy due to non-payment of premiums.
*   **Policyholder:** The owner of this Policy, who has rights and responsibilities outlined herein.
*   **Premium:** The payment required by the Policyholder to keep the Policy in force.
*   **Surrender Value:** The Cash Value, less any outstanding policy loans and surrender charges (if applicable), that is paid to the Policyholder upon voluntarily terminating the Policy.

## 4. Main Content

### 4.1 Death Benefit

Upon receipt of satisfactory proof of the Insured's death while this Policy is in force, We will pay the Death Benefit, less any outstanding loans and accrued interest, to the designated Beneficiary(ies). The Death Benefit amount is specified in the Policy Declarations.

### 4.2 Cash Value Accumulation

This Policy accumulates Cash Value on a guaranteed basis. The Cash Value grows over time at a stated minimum interest rate, as specified in the Policy Specifications. This accumulation is tax-deferred under current tax laws. The Cash Value may be used for policy loans or surrendered for its Surrender Value.

### 4.3 Premiums

The Policyholder agrees to pay regular premiums as set forth in the Policy Declarations. Premiums are due on the due date specified. A grace period of thirty-one (31) days is allowed for the payment of each premium after the first. If the premium is not paid within the grace period, and the Policy has insufficient Cash Value to fund an Automatic Premium Loan (if selected), the Policy will lapse.

### 4.4 Policy Loans

The Policyholder may borrow against the Cash Value of this Policy, provided the Policy is in force and has accumulated sufficient Cash Value.
*   **Maximum Loan Amount:** The maximum amount that can be borrowed is generally 90% of the Policy's Cash Value, less any existing indebtedness.
*   **Interest Rate:** Policy loans accrue interest at a fixed or adjustable rate as specified in the Policy Specifications. Current interest rate for new loans is 5% annual simple interest.
*   **Repayment:** Loan repayment is flexible. Unpaid interest will be added to the loan principal annually. Outstanding loans and interest will reduce the Death Benefit payable to Beneficiary(ies) and the Surrender Value if the Policy is terminated.
*   **Impact of Loan on Cash Value:** While a loan is outstanding, the portion of Cash Value securing the loan may earn a reduced or no interest rate.

### 4.5 Non-Forfeiture Options

If premium payments cease after Cash Value has accumulated, the Policyholder may elect one of the following non-forfeiture options within sixty (60) days of the premium due date:

*   **Option A: Cash Surrender Value:** Receive the Net Surrender Value of the Policy. This terminates the Policy.
*   **Option B: Reduced Paid-Up Insurance:** The Net Surrender Value is used as a single premium to purchase a new, smaller amount of Whole Life insurance that is fully paid-up. No further premiums are required, and the Cash Value continues to grow.
*   **Option C: Extended Term Insurance:** The Net Surrender Value is used as a single premium to purchase a Term Life insurance policy for the same Death Benefit amount as the original Policy, for a specified period. No further premiums are required. This option does not accumulate Cash Value.

If no option is selected, Option C (Extended Term Insurance) will automatically be applied after the grace period expires, provided there is sufficient Cash Value.

### 4.6 Riders and Endorsements

Any riders or endorsements attached to this Policy and listed in the Policy Declarations are an integral part of this Contract and supersede any conflicting provisions contained within the main body of the Policy. Examples include:
*   Waiver of Premium Rider
*   Accidental Death Benefit Rider
*   Guaranteed Insurability Rider

## 5. Requirements and Criteria

### 5.1 Policyholder Eligibility

*   Must be at least 18 years of age at the time of application.
*   Must have insurable interest in the life of the Insured.

### 5.2 Insured Eligibility

*   Must meet underwriting requirements, including medical review and financial assessment. Age limits typically 0-85 at issue.
*   Must consent to the insurance coverage.

### 5.3 Premium Payment Requirements

*   Premiums must be paid in full on or before the due date.
*   Acceptable payment methods include bank draft, check, credit card, or electronic funds transfer.

### 5.4 Claim Submission Requirements for Death Benefit

*   Original Policy Contract (if available) or certified copy of the Policy Declarations.
*   Certified copy of the Insured's Death Certificate.
*   Claimant's Statement form, completed and signed by the Beneficiary(ies).
*   Any other documentation reasonably requested by the Company to establish proof of death and beneficiary entitlement.

## 6. Procedures

### 6.1 Policy Application Process

1.  **Application Submission:** Policyholder completes and signs an application form, including details of the Insured.
2.  **Underwriting Review:** The Company reviews the application, medical records (if required), and other relevant information to assess the risk. This process typically takes 10-30 business days.
3.  **Policy Offer:** If approved, the Company issues a policy offer detailing the coverage, premiums, and any special conditions.
4.  **Acceptance and Initial Premium:** The Policyholder accepts the offer by signing the Policy Declarations and paying the initial premium.
5.  **Policy Issuance:** The Policy is officially issued with an Effective Date, and the Contract is delivered to the Policyholder.

### 6.2 Changing Beneficiary

1.  **Request Form:** The Policyholder must submit a completed Beneficiary Change Request Form.
2.  **Designation:** The form must clearly identify the new primary and contingent Beneficiary(ies) and their relationship to the Insured.
3.  **Signature:** The form must be signed by the Policyholder.
4.  **Effective Date:** The change becomes effective on the date the Company records the change, subject to any prior payments made by the Company in good faith before recording the change.

### 6.3 Requesting a Policy Loan

1.  **Loan Request Form:** Policyholder submits a Policy Loan Request Form.
2.  **Policy in Force:** Policy must be in force and have sufficient Cash Value.
3.  **Processing:** Loans are typically processed within 5-7 business days of receiving a complete request.
4.  **Disbursement:** Funds are disbursed via check or electronic transfer to the Policyholder.

## 7. Exceptions

### 7.1 Incontestability

This Policy shall be incontestable after it has been in force during the lifetime of the Insured for two (2) years from its Issue Date, except for non-payment of premiums. Any statements made in the application, in the absence of fraud, shall be deemed representations and not warranties.

### 7.2 Misstatement of Age or Sex

If the age or sex of the Insured has been misstated, the amount payable under this Policy will be such as the premium paid would have purchased at the correct age and sex according to the Companyâ€™s rates at the Issue Date.

### 7.3 Reinstatement

A Policy that has lapsed due to non-payment of premiums may be reinstated, subject to the following:
*   Application for reinstatement within five (5) years of the lapse date.
*   Payment of all overdue premiums with interest.
*   Proof of insurability satisfactory to the Company.
*   Repayment or reinstatement of any policy loans that existed at the time of lapse, with interest.

### 7.4 Assignment

The Policyholder may assign rights and benefits under this Policy to another party. Any assignment must be made in writing and filed with the Company. The Company is not responsible for the validity of any assignment.

### 7.5 War Clause

If the death of the Insured is a direct or indirect result of war, declared or undeclared, or any act incident to war, while the Insured is serving in the armed forces of any country, international organization, or combination of countries, the Company's liability will be limited to the premiums paid, less any outstanding loans. This clause applies only if death occurs within two (2) years of the Issue Date of the Policy or within two (2) years of the effective date of an increase in coverage for the increased amount.

## 8. Examples

### 8.1 Reduced Paid-Up Illustration

A Policy with a $100,000 Death Benefit and $15,000 Cash Value lapses. The Policyholder elects Reduced Paid-Up insurance. Assuming the Net Surrender Value of $15,000 is sufficient to purchase $40,000 of paid-up Whole Life insurance based on the Insured's age, the Policy will convert to a $40,000 paid-up policy. No further premiums are due, and the $40,000 will be paid at death.

### 8.2 Policy Loan Scenario

Policyholder has a $20,000 Cash Value and needs $5,000 for an emergency. They request a policy loan. The Company approves the loan at a 5% annual interest rate. The Death Benefit remains $100,000. If the Insured dies with the $5,000 loan outstanding and $250 in accrued interest, the Beneficiary will receive $100,000 - $5,000 - $250 = $94,750.

## 9. Decision Logic

### 9.1 Premium Payment Status

*   **Payment received on or before due date:** Policy remains In Force.
*   **Payment received within 31-day grace period:** Policy remains In Force. Late payment fee may apply.
*   **Payment not received within 31-day grace period, with sufficient Cash Value and Auto Premium Loan elected:** Automatic Premium Loan is processed, Policy remains In Force, loan balance increases. Policyholder notified of loan.
*   **Payment not received within 31-day grace period, insufficient Cash Value or Auto Premium Loan not elected:** Policy Lapses. Non-forfeiture options triggered.

### 9.2 Death Benefit Payout Criteria

*   **Policy In Force at time of death:** Full Death Benefit paid, less any outstanding loans/interest, subject to exclusions.
*   **Death due to suicide within 2 years (from Issue Date or increase effective date):** Premiums paid refunded, less outstanding loans.
*   **Death due to war within 2 years (from Issue Date or increase effective date):** Premiums paid refunded, less outstanding loans.
*   **Beneficiary designation unclear/disputed:** Funds held in escrow until a court order or satisfactory agreement is reached among claimants.

## 11. Timelines

*   **Grace Period for Premiums:** 31 days from the due date.
*   **Policy Loan Processing:** Typically 5-7 business days from receipt of a complete request.
*   **Death Benefit Claim Processing:** Within 30 days of receiving all required documentation, assuming no disputes or extraordinary circumstances.
*   **Reinstatement Application Period:** Up to 5 years from the date of lapse.
*   **Incontestability Period:** 2 years from Issue Date (or effective date for increased coverage).
*   **Suicide Exclusion Period:** 2 years from Issue Date (or effective date for increased coverage).
