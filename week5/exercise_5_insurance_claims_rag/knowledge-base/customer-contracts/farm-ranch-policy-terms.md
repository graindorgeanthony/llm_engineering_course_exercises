# Farm and Ranch Insurance Policy Terms and Conditions

## 1. Overview and Purpose

This document outlines the comprehensive terms, conditions, coverages, exclusions, and endorsements for Farm and Ranch Multi-Peril Insurance Policies issued by AgriShield Insurance Group. The purpose of this policy is to provide financial protection against defined perils for property, liability, and other risks inherent in agricultural operations, including farms, ranches, and associated dwellings. This document forms a binding contract between the named Insured and AgriShield Insurance Group.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all standard Farm and Ranch Multi-Peril Insurance Policies (Policy Form AR-MP-2023) issued to individuals, partnerships, or corporate entities operating an active farm or ranch within the United States. It covers designated property, assets, operations, and liabilities described in the policy declarations.

### 2.1. Inclusions
*   Dwellings and personal property on the insured premises.
*   Farm structures (e.g., barns, silos, fences).
*   Farm personal property (e.g., machinery, equipment, livestock, stored crops).
*   Farm and personal liability.
*   Specific endorsements as listed in the Declarations Page.

### 2.2. Exclusions
*   Properties or operations not explicitly declared and scheduled.
*   Commercial operations unrelated to farming/ranching unless specifically endorsed.
*   Hazards typically covered by separate policies (e.g., Worker's Compensation for employees, flood insurance, earthquake insurance) unless specifically added by endorsement.
*   Intentional acts, war, nuclear hazard.
*   Loss caused by normal wear and tear, deterioration, rust, mold, or inherent vice.

## 3. Definitions

*   **Insured:** The person(s) or entity named in the Declarations Page to whom this policy is issued.
*   **Insured Premises:** The land, buildings, and structures specified in the Declarations Page where farming or ranching operations occur.
*   **Farm Personal Property:** Livestock, farm machinery, equipment, tools, grain, feed, silage, and other agricultural products and supplies owned by the Insured or for which the Insured is legally responsible.
*   **Coverage Period:** The period beginning and ending on the dates specified in the Declarations Page, during which coverage is active.
*   **Peril:** A cause of loss such as fire, lightning, windstorm, hail, theft, explosion, etc., as defined within the policy.
*   **Deductible:** The amount of loss or damage that the Insured is responsible for paying before the insurance company pays its share.

## 4. Coverage Sections

### 4.1. Section A: Dwelling and Personal Property

Covers the dwelling located on the insured premises, including attached structures, and the personal property of the Insured and resident family members.

#### 4.1.1. Dwelling Coverage (Coverage A)
*   **Perils Covered:** Named perils (fire, lightning, windstorm, hail, explosion, riot, aircraft, vehicles, smoke, vandalism, theft, falling objects, weight of ice/snow/sleet, sudden/accidental tear apart/cracking/burning/bulging of heating/AC/sprinkler system, freezing of plumbing, sudden/accidental discharge of water/steam) as specified in Declarations.
*   **Valuation:** Replacement Cost for structures, provided repairs commence within 180 days of loss. If repairs exceed 180 days, Actual Cash Value (ACV) applies until repairs are complete.
*   **Deductible:** As specified in the Declarations Page, typically $1,000 to $2,500 per occurrence.

#### 4.1.2. Personal Property Coverage (Coverage B)
*   **Perils Covered:** Same named perils as Dwelling Coverage A.
*   **Valuation:** Actual Cash Value (ACV) unless Replacement Cost Endorsement B-RC is active.
*   **Limits:** Typically 50% of Coverage A limit, with sub-limits for specific items (e.g., $2,500 for jewelry, $200 for money).
*   **Off-Premises Coverage:** Up to 10% of Coverage B limit for personal property temporarily removed from the insured premises for up to 30 days.

### 4.2. Section B: Farm Structures

Covers barns, sheds, silos, fences, corrals, and other structures used in farming or ranching operations.

#### 4.2.1. Covered Perils
*   Named perils (fire, lightning, windstorm, hail, explosion, riot, aircraft, vehicles, smoke, vandalism, theft). Specific flood and earthquake exclusions apply unless endorsed.
*   Farm structures are typically covered at Actual Cash Value (ACV). Replacement Cost valuation available via Endorsement FS-RC.

#### 4.2.2. Requirements
*   All structures must be listed individually on the Declarations Page with a specific insurable value. Unlisted structures are not covered.
*   Structures must be maintained in good repair. Damage resulting from neglect (e.g., unrepaired structural weaknesses prior to a storm) is excluded.

### 4.3. Section C: Farm Personal Property

Covers machinery, equipment, livestock, stored crops, and other personal property integral to the agricultural operation.

#### 4.3.1. Farm Machinery and Equipment
*   **Perils Covered:** Named perils (fire, lightning, windstorm, hail, collision, overturn, theft, vandalism).
*   **Valuation:** Actual Cash Value (ACV). Replacement Cost endorsement (FME-RC) available for items less than 5 years old.
*   **Deductible:** Usually $500 to $1,000 per occurrence, as specified.

#### 4.3.2. Livestock
*   **Perils Covered:** Death resulting directly from fire, lightning, windstorm, hail, explosion, collision (with vehicle not owned/operated by Insured), attack by wild animals, collapse of buildings, drowning, electrocution.
*   **Exclusions:** Disease, sickness, calving complications, governmental destruction order, market fluctuation, mysterious disappearance.
*   **Valuation:** Actual Cash Value (ACV) up to a maximum per-head limit (e.g., cattle $2,000/head, sheep $300/head, horses $5,000/head) listed in the Declarations. Higher limits require specific scheduling and valuation for individual animals.

#### 4.3.3. Stored Crops, Grain, and Feed
*   **Perils Covered:** Fire, lightning, theft, windstorm, hail damage while stored in an enclosed building or structure.
*   **Exclusions:** Damage from insects, rodents, inherent vice, natural spoilage, market loss.
*   **Valuation:** Market value at time of loss.

### 4.4. Section D: Farm and Personal Liability

Provides coverage for bodily injury or property damage to others arising from farm operations or personal activities on the insured premises.

#### 4.4.1. Limits of Liability
*   **Combined Single Limit (CSL):** Typically $500,000 or $1,000,000 per occurrence.
*   **Medical Payments:** $5,000 per person.

#### 4.4.2. Coverage Details
*   **Bodily Injury/Property Damage:** Covers sums the Insured becomes legally obligated to pay due to bodily injury or property damage caused by an occurrence on the insured premises or arising from farm operations.
*   **Personal and Advertising Injury:** Covers specific offenses (e.g., libel, slander, false arrest) committed in the course of advertising the Insured's goods, products, or services.
*   **Exclusions:** Expected/intended injury, contractual liability, worker's compensation, pollution, aircraft/watercraft/auto liability (unless specifically endorsed), professional services, employee injury.

## 5. General Policy Conditions

### 5.1. Insured's Duties After Loss
In the event of a loss, the Insured must:
1.  Give prompt notice to AgriShield Insurance Group, including policy number and circumstances of loss.
2.  Protect the property from further damage.
3.  Cooperate with AgriShield in the investigation and settlement of the claim.
4.  Submit a signed, sworn Proof of Loss form within 60 days of the company's request.
5.  Provide records and documents as reasonably requested by AgriShield.

### 5.2. Valuation and Settlement
*   Unless otherwise specified, losses are settled at Actual Cash Value (ACV), which is replacement cost less depreciation.
*   The maximum amount payable for any loss is the limit of liability shown in the Declarations Page for that coverage, less the applicable deductible.

### 5.3. Other Insurance
If other valid and collectible insurance applies to a loss covered by this policy, AgriShield will pay only its proportion of the loss.

### 5.4. Cancellation and Non-Renewal
*   **Insured's Right to Cancel:** The Insured may cancel this policy at any time by mailing or delivering written notice to AgriShield.
*   **AgriShield's Right to Cancel/Non-Renew:** AgriShield may cancel or non-renew this policy by giving written notice to the Insured at least 30 days prior to the effective date of cancellation/non-renewal (or 10 days for non-payment of premium or material misrepresentation). Reasons for cancellation include: non-payment of premium, material misrepresentation, substantial change in risk.

## 6. Endorsements

The specific endorsements attached to the policy Declarations Page modify, add, or delete certain terms, conditions, coverages, or exclusions. Common endorsements include:

*   **B-RC:** Dwelling and Personal Property Replacement Cost
*   **FS-RC:** Farm Structures Replacement Cost
*   **FME-RC:** Farm Machinery and Equipment Replacement Cost
*   **AR-CR:** Crop Revenue for specified crops (e.g., corn, wheat, soybeans)
*   **AR-FP:** Farm Pollution Liability
*   **AR-HO:** Home Office/Business Coverage (e.g., for direct sales of farm products)
*   **AR-AW:** Additional Water/Sewer Backup Coverage

## 7. Claim Filing Process

1.  **Immediate Notification:** Report loss within 72 hours via phone ([Toll-Free Number]) or online portal.
2.  **Claim Form Submission:** Complete and submit initial claim form, providing details of the loss.
3.  **Adjuster Assignment:** An AgriShield claims adjuster will be assigned within 2 business days.
4.  **Inspection and Documentation:** Cooperate with the adjuster for property inspection and provide all requested documentation (e.g., photos, receipts, police reports, livestock veterinary records).
5.  **Proof of Loss:** Submit a signed and notarized Proof of Loss form within 60 days of being requested.
6.  **Settlement:** Upon agreement, payment will be issued typically within 15-30 days after Proof of Loss acceptance.

## 8. Exceptions

*   **Emergency Repairs:** In emergency situations where immediate repairs are necessary to prevent further damage (e.g., tarping a damaged roof after a storm), the Insured may proceed with temporary repairs up to $2,500 without prior approval, provided they document the damage and costs (photos, receipts).
*   **Scheduled Items:** Specific high-value items (e.g., antique tractors, prize-winning livestock, unique equipment) can be scheduled individually with agreed-upon values, overriding standard per-head or ACV limits for those specific items. This requires a specific endorsement and appraisal.
*   **Force Majeure:** AgriShield Insurance Group is not liable for delays in claim processing beyond its control due to acts of God, war, civil unrest, or governmental action. However, efforts will be made to process claims as quickly as reasonably possible under such circumstances.
