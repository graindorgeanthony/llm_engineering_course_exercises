# Travel Insurance Policy Terms and Conditions

## Overview/Purpose

This document outlines the comprehensive terms and conditions of the Travel Insurance Policy (hereinafter, "the Policy") offered by GlobalGuard Insurance Group. Its purpose is to clearly define the contractual agreement between the policyholder and GlobalGuard Insurance Group, specifying the scope of coverage, exclusions, claims procedures, and responsibilities of all parties involved. This Policy is designed to provide financial protection against unforeseen events that may disrupt or impact a planned trip.

## Scope

This Policy provides coverage for insured events occurring during the specified Period of Insurance and within the defined Geographical Limits as stated in the Policy Schedule. It applies to individuals, couples, or families (as defined in the Policy Schedule) who have purchased this travel insurance.

**Included:**
*   Emergency medical expenses incurred during travel.
*   Trip cancellation or interruption due to covered reasons.
*   Lost, stolen, or damaged baggage and personal effects.
*   Travel delays or missed connections under specific conditions.
*   Emergency evacuation and repatriation.

**Excluded:**
*   Pre-existing medical conditions not declared and accepted by GlobalGuard Insurance Group.
*   Travel to destinations under government advisory warnings (e.g., "Do Not Travel").
*   Acts of war, terrorism, or civil unrest (unless specifically covered by an endorsement).
*   Participation in professional sports or hazardous activities without prior approval and specific endorsement.
*   Losses arising from reckless behavior or willful misconduct by the policyholder.

## Definitions

*   **Policyholder:** The individual or entity named in the Policy Schedule as the primary insured.
*   **Insured Person(s):** The Policyholder and any other individuals specifically named in the Policy Schedule.
*   **Period of Insurance:** The duration of coverage, commencing on the Start Date and ending on the End Date, as specified in the Policy Schedule.
*   **Trip:** The journey commencing when the Insured Person(s) leaves their home and ending upon their return to their home, within the Period of Insurance.
*   **Loss:** Financial detriment incurred due to an insured event.
*   **Pre-existing Medical Condition:** Any illness, injury, or medical condition for which the Insured Person(s) has received diagnosis, medical advice, or treatment within 24 months prior to the Policy's effective date, irrespective of whether symptoms were present.
*   **Reasonable and Customary Charges:** Charges for medical care, services, or supplies that are consistent with the prevailing rates charged by similar providers for similar services in the geographic area where the services are rendered.
*   **Excess/Deductible:** The first amount of any claimable loss that the Insured Person(s) must pay per incident, as stated in the Policy Schedule.

## Main Content

### Section 1: Medical Emergency Coverage

#### 1.1 Emergency Medical Expenses

GlobalGuard Insurance Group will reimburse reasonable and customary charges for emergency medical treatment received by an Insured Person during the Trip for a sudden and unexpected illness or injury that first occurred during the Period of Insurance.

*   **Coverage Limit:** Up to USD 1,000,000 per Insured Person, per Trip.
*   **Included Services:** Hospitalization, physician's fees, diagnostic tests (e.g., X-rays, lab work), prescription medications obtained during the emergency, and emergency dental treatment (up to USD 500 for acute pain relief).
*   **Exclusions:** Routine medical check-ups, cosmetic procedures, experimental treatments, treatment of chronic conditions not arising from an acute emergency, and treatment sought after returning to the country of domicile.
*   **Pre-requisite:** For claims exceeding USD 1,000, or requiring admission to a hospital or surgery, GlobalGuard Insurance Group's 24/7 Emergency Assistance Service **MUST** be contacted prior to treatment, if medically feasible.

#### 1.2 Emergency Medical Evacuation and Repatriation

If an Insured Person suffers a medical emergency abroad and GlobalGuard Insurance Group's medical team determines that local medical facilities are inadequate, we will arrange and cover the cost of medically necessary evacuation to the nearest suitable medical facility or repatriation to their home country.

*   **Coverage Limit:** Unlimited, when arranged by GlobalGuard Insurance Group.
*   **Decision Logic:** Evacuation/repatriation decision rests solely with GlobalGuard Insurance Group's medical director, based on medical necessity and availability of appropriate medical care.
*   **Evidence:** Medical reports from attending physicians, recommendation from GlobalGuard Insurance Group's medical team.

### Section 2: Trip Cancellation & Interruption Coverage

#### 2.1 Trip Cancellation

GlobalGuard Insurance Group will reimburse non-refundable, pre-paid travel expenses if the Insured Person must cancel their Trip prior to departure due to a covered reason.

*   **Coverage Limit:** Up to the sum insured per Insured Person, as specified in the Policy Schedule.
*   **Covered Reasons:**
    *   Sudden, unexpected illness, injury, or death of the Insured Person, a Close Family Member, or a Traveling Companion.
    *   Sickness or injury of a service animal that prevents the Insured Person from traveling.
    *   Jury duty or a subpoena for a court appearance for the Insured Person.
    *   Official government warning against travel to the planned destination issued after the Policy was purchased (excluding advisories present at the time of purchase).
    *   Significant adverse weather events preventing travel, certified by the common carrier.
    *   Home becoming uninhabitable due to fire, flood, or natural disaster within 10 days of departure.
*   **Evidence Required:** Medical certificates, death certificates, court summons, official weather reports, property damage reports, booking confirmations, proof of payment for non-refundable expenses.

#### 2.2 Trip Interruption

GlobalGuard Insurance Group will reimburse for the unused portion of pre-paid, non-refundable travel arrangements and additional transportation expenses to return home if the Trip is interrupted due to a covered reason.

*   **Coverage Limit:** Up to the sum insured per Insured Person, as specified in the Policy Schedule.
*   **Covered Reasons:** Same as for Trip Cancellation.
*   **Evidence Required:** Similar to Trip Cancellation, plus receipts for additional return transportation.

### Section 3: Baggage and Personal Effects Coverage

#### 3.1 Lost, Stolen, or Damaged Baggage

GlobalGuard Insurance Group will reimburse for the loss, theft, or damage to personal baggage and effects during the Trip.

*   **Coverage Limit:** Up to USD 2,500 per Insured Person, with a single item limit of USD 500 (unless higher limits for specific items are endorsed in the Policy Schedule).
*   **Wear and Tear:** Not covered.
*   **Excluded Items:** Money, securities, credit cards, valuable documents (passports, tickets), business samples, mobile phones, eyewear (unless damaged with bag), hearing aids, artificial limbs.
*   **Timelines:** Claims must be reported to the common carrier (airline, bus company) or police within 24 hours of discovery of loss/damage.
*   **Evidence Required:** Property Irregularity Report (PIR) from common carrier, police report (for theft), original purchase receipts for lost/damaged items, repair quotes if applicable.

#### 3.2 Delayed Baggage

If checked baggage is delayed by a common carrier for more than 12 consecutive hours during the Trip, GlobalGuard Insurance Group will reimburse for essential emergency purchases.

*   **Coverage Limit:** Up to USD 200 per Insured Person.
*   **Evidence Required:** Property Irregularity Report (PIR) detailing delay duration, receipts for essential items purchased (e.g., toiletries, basic clothing), proof of baggage delivery date.

## Requirements/Criteria for Claims

To file a claim, the Policyholder must:
1.  Notify GlobalGuard Insurance Group as soon as reasonably possible, typically within 30 days of the incident.
2.  Provide all requested documentation truthfully and completely.
3.  Cooperate fully with any investigation conducted by GlobalGuard Insurance Group.
4.  Mitigate loss where possible (e.g., seek alternative, cost-effective transport).
5.  Maintain all original receipts and evidentiary documents.

## Procedures/Steps for Filing a Claim

1.  **Contact GlobalGuard Insurance Group:** Access the online claims portal or call the claims hotline at +1-800-555-0199.
2.  **Submit Initial Notification:** Provide Policyholder details, Policy Number, date of incident, and a brief description of the claim.
3.  **Receive Claim Form and Instructions:** GlobalGuard Insurance Group will send a claim form along with a checklist of required documents.
4.  **Gather Documentation:** Collect all necessary evidence as listed under relevant sections (e.g., medical reports, receipts, police reports, airline PIRs).
5.  **Complete Claim Form:** Fill out the claim form accurately and entirely.
6.  **Submit Claim:** Upload all documents through the online portal or mail them to the provided address.
7.  **Claim Assessment:** A claims adjuster will review the submission. This process typically takes 10-15 business days.
8.  **Decision and Payment:** Upon approval, payment will be issued via bank transfer or check. If denied, a detailed explanation will be provided.

## Exceptions

*   Claims arising from travel against medical advice or where the Insured Person was awaiting diagnosis for symptoms.
*   Losses due to an Insured Person's own criminal act or gross negligence.
*   Losses recoverable from any other source (e.g., airline compensation, credit card insurance).
*   Claims for expenses deliberately incurred that are beyond what is reasonable and necessary for treatment or resolution of the incident.

## Examples

**Example 1: Trip Cancellation**
*   **Scenario:** Jane Doe purchased a policy for a trip to Italy. Three days before departure, her father (a Close Family Member) suffered a sudden heart attack and was hospitalized. Jane cancelled her non-refundable flight and hotel.
*   **Decision Logic:** Her father's sudden, unexpected illness qualifies as a covered reason under Section 2.1.
*   **Evidence:** Medical certificate from her father's doctor, his hospitalization records, flight & hotel booking confirmations, proof of non-refundable payments.

**Example 2: Emergency Medical Treatment**
*   **Scenario:** John Smith, while on a trip in Thailand, developed acute appendicitis and required emergency surgery. He contacted GlobalGuard Emergency Assistance who approved his surgery at a local hospital.
*   **Decision Logic:** Acute, unexpected illness requiring emergency treatment is covered under Section 1.1. Pre-authorization from GlobalGuard confirms coverage.
*   **Evidence:** Hospital bills, surgeon's report, receipts for prescription medication, contact log with GlobalGuard Emergency Assistance.

## Timelines

*   **Claim Notification:** Within 30 days of the incident's occurrence or as soon as reasonably possible.
*   **Document Submission:** Within 90 days of claim notification.
*   **Claim Processing:** GlobalGuard aims to process complete claims within 15 business days of receiving all required documentation.
*   **Payment:** Issued within 5 business days of claim approval.
