# Communication Templates for Claimants

## Overview
This document provides standardized letter and email templates designed for efficient and consistent communication with claimants throughout various stages of the claims process. Adherence to these templates ensures clarity, compliance, and a professional claimant experience. These templates are essential for maintaining a unified voice and expediting claim management by providing pre-approved messaging for common scenarios.

## Scope
This reference document applies to all claims personnel, including adjusters, claims processors, and customer service representatives, who are responsible for communicating with claimants regarding their open claims. It covers standard communications from initial acknowledgment to claim resolution and specific requests.
Excluded from this scope are internal communications, legal correspondence initiated by the legal department, and highly personalized or complex dispute resolution communications that require custom drafting.

## Definitions
*   **Claimant:** The individual or entity filing a claim.
*   **Claim ID:** A unique identifier assigned to each claim for tracking purposes.
*   **Adjudication:** The process of evaluating a claim based on policy terms and evidence to determine eligibility and extent of coverage.
*   **Proof of Loss (PoL):** A formal statement made by the insured claimant to the insurer, detailing the damages or losses incurred.
*   **Acknowledgment:** Initial notification to the claimant confirming receipt of their claim.
*   **Declination:** Formal notification to the claimant that their claim has been denied.
*   **Subrogation:** The right for an insurer to pursue a third party that caused an insurance loss to the insured.

## Template Usage Guidelines

1.  **Standardization:** Always use the approved templates provided herein. Do not modify content without explicit approval from a Claims Supervisor or the Compliance Department.
2.  **Personalization:** While templates offer a base, always replace bracketed placeholders (e.g., `[Claimant Name]`, `[Claim ID]`, `[Date]`) with specific claim details.
3.  **Accuracy:** Ensure all personalized information (dates, amounts, references) is accurate and consistent with the claim record.
4.  **Timeliness:** Send communications within specified service level agreement (SLA) timeframes.
5.  **Language:** Maintain a professional, empathetic, and clear tone. Avoid jargon where possible.
6.  **Record Keeping:** All outgoing communications must be logged in the claims management system (CMS) with the exact date and content sent.

## General Communication Principles

*   **Clarity:** Messages should be easy to understand.
*   **Conciseness:** Get straight to the point without unnecessary detail.
*   **Professionalism:** Maintain a respectful and empathetic tone.
*   **Action-Oriented:** Clearly state any required actions from the claimant or what they can expect next.
*   **Contact Information:** Always include a direct contact (phone number, email) for the claims handler or department.

## Communication Templates

### 1. Initial Claim Acknowledgment (Email/Letter)

**Purpose:** To confirm receipt of a new claim, provide the claim ID, and outline next steps.
**Trigger:** Claim received and entered into CMS.
**Timeline:** Within 1 business day of claim receipt.

```
Subject: Your Claim Has Been Received - Claim ID: [Claim ID]

Dear [Claimant Name],

Thank you for contacting us. We confirm receipt of your claim, filed on [Date of Claim Filing].

Your Claim ID is: **[Claim ID]**. Please use this reference number in all future correspondence with us.

We understand this may be a challenging time, and we are here to help you through the process. Our team is now reviewing your information.

Hereâ€™s what you can expect next:
1.  A dedicated claims adjuster, [Adjuster Name, if assigned], will be assigned to your case and may contact you within [Number] business days to discuss your claim in more detail and explain the next steps.
2.  We may request additional documentation or information to help us with our assessment.

In the meantime, if you have any questions, please do not hesitate to contact your assigned adjuster at [Adjuster Phone Number] or reply to this email. For general inquiries, you can reach our Claims Department at [General Claims Phone Number].

Thank you for your patience.

Sincerely,

The [Company Name] Claims Team
[Company Website]
```

### 2. Request for Additional Information (Email/Letter)

**Purpose:** To request specific documents or details necessary for claim processing.
**Trigger:** Insufficient information received to proceed with adjudication.
**Timeline:** As soon as the need for additional information is identified.

```
Subject: Action Required: Information Needed for Claim ID: [Claim ID]

Dear [Claimant Name],

Regarding your claim, Claim ID: [Claim ID], filed on [Date of Claim Filing], we require some additional information to continue with our assessment.

To process your claim efficiently, please provide the following:
*   [Specific Document/Information 1, e.g., Itemized repair estimate for damaged property]
*   [Specific Document/Information 2, e.g., Police report for incident dated XX/XX/XXXX]
*   [Specific Document/Information 3, e.g., Medical records pertaining to XYX injury]

Please submit this information by [Date - typically 10-14 business days from send date] to avoid delays in processing your claim. You can submit documents via:
*   Email: [Claims Email Address] (please reference Claim ID [Claim ID] in the subject line)
*   Mail: [Company Claims Mailing Address]
*   Online Portal: [Link to Claimant Portal, if applicable]

If you have any questions or require assistance in gathering these documents, please contact your claims adjuster, [Adjuster Name], at [Adjuster Phone Number].

Thank you for your prompt attention to this matter.

Sincerely,

The [Company Name] Claims Team
```

### 3. Claim Status Update (Email/Letter)

**Purpose:** To proactively inform the claimant about the progress of their claim, especially if there's a delay or significant milestone.
**Trigger:** No significant activity for [X] business days, or upon reaching a major internal review milestone.
**Timeline:** Every [X] business days or after significant internal action.

```
Subject: Update on Your Claim - Claim ID: [Claim ID]

Dear [Claimant Name],

This email provides an update on the status of your claim, Claim ID: [Claim ID], filed on [Date of Claim Filing].

Current Status: [Choose one: Under review / Awaiting requested documents / In final stages of assessment / Adjuster has completed initial investigation]

[Conditional content based on status:]
*   _If Under Review:_ Our claims team is actively reviewing all submitted documentation. We anticipate completing this phase by [Estimated Date], at which point we will contact you with further information or a decision.
*   _If Awaiting Documents:_ We are still awaiting the documents requested on [Date of Request]. Please refer to our previous communication for details on what is needed. Your prompt submission will help us process your claim without further delay.
*   _If In Final Stages:_ Your claim is now in the final stages of assessment. We are preparing our decision and will communicate it to you by [Estimated Date - typically 3-5 business days].
*   _If Adjuster Completed Investigation:_ Your assigned adjuster, [Adjuster Name], has completed the initial investigation. The findings are currently undergoing internal review.

We appreciate your patience throughout this process. Should you have any questions, please reach out to your adjuster, [Adjuster Name], at [Adjuster Phone Number] or reply to this email.

Sincerely,

The [Company Name] Claims Team
```

**Purpose:** To formally approve a claim and present a settlement offer.
**Trigger:** Claim adjudication complete and approved for payment.
**Timeline:** Within 2 business days of final approval.

```

**Timeline:** Within 2 business days of final declination decision.

```

Dear [Claimant Name],

The reason(s) for this decision are as follows:
1.  **[Reason 1, e.g., Policy Exclusions]:** The loss falls under the exclusion for [Specific Exclusion Term] as outlined in Section [Policy Section Number] of your policy document, which states [Quote relevant policy language].

### 6. Subrogation Notification (Email/Letter)

**Purpose:** To inform the claimant about the subrogation process if applicable.
**Trigger:** Claim paid, and a third party is identified as liable.
**Timeline:** Within 5 business days of subrogation initiation.

```
Subject: Subrogation Regarding Your Claim - Claim ID: [Claim ID]

Dear [Claimant Name],

This letter is to inform you about the subrogation efforts being pursued in connection with your claim, Claim ID: [Claim ID], for the incident that occurred on [Date of Incident].

As you know, your claim for damages amounting to $[Amount Paid] was paid on [Date of Payment]. Through our investigation, it has been determined that [Third-Party Name/Company] may be responsible for the damages.

We are now initiating efforts to recover the paid amount from [Third-Party Name/Company] on your behalf. This process, known as subrogation, allows us to seek reimbursement from the responsible party.

What this means for you:
*   We will directly handle all correspondence and negotiations with the at-fault party or their insurer.
*   You are not required to take any further action regarding the recovery, unless we specifically contact you for additional information or testimony.
*   If your deductible of $[Deductible Amount] was applied to your claim, we will also attempt to recover this amount for you. If successful, your deductible will be reimbursed to you. Please note that recovery of the deductible cannot be guaranteed and depends on the outcome of the subrogation effort.

We will keep you informed of any significant developments in the subrogation process. If you have any questions, please contact [Subrogation Specialist Name/Claims Adjuster Name] at [Phone Number] or [Email Address].

Sincerely,

The [Company Name] Subrogation Department
```

## Exceptions
*   **Urgent Cases:** For claims involving severe injury, catastrophic loss, or regulatory mandates requiring immediate verbal notification, follow-up written communication should still adhere to appropriate templates as soon as practically possible.
*   **Legal Hold:** If a claim is under legal review or litigation, all communication with the claimant must be approved by the Legal Department prior to sending. These cases will typically deviate from standard templates.
*   **High-Value Claims:** Claims exceeding $[Threshold Amount] may require additional review by a Claims Supervisor before templates are sent, particularly for settlement offers or declinations, to ensure all details are precisely accurate and legally sound.

## Evidence/Documentation
*   All sent communications (emails and letters) derived from these templates must be saved as PDFs or directly logged within the claims management system (CMS) under the respective Claim ID.
*   Confirmation of delivery (e.g., mail tracking numbers, email read receipts if enabled) should also be recorded.

## Timelines
*   **Initial Acknowledgment:** Within 1 business day of claim receipt.
*   **Request for Information:** Within 2 business days of identifying information gap.
*   **Claim Status Update:** Minimum every 10 business days if claim is pending.
*   **Claim Approval/Declination:** Within 2 business days of final decision.
*   **Subrogation Notification:** Within 5 business days of initiating subrogation efforts.

Adherence to these templates and guidelines is mandatory for all claims personnel.
