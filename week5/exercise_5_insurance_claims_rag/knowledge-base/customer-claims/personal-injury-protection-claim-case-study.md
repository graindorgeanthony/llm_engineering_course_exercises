# Personal Injury Protection Claim Case Study: Jane Doe

## Overview/Purpose
This document details a Personal Injury Protection (PIP) claim filed by Jane Doe following a motor vehicle accident. It serves as a case study to demonstrate the processing, evaluation, and resolution of PIP claims, focusing on medical bill adjudication, lost wage calculations, and overall claim management. This case study highlights typical challenges and the decision logic applied by claim adjusters.

## Scope
This case study covers the period from the accident date (December 1, 2022) through the final settlement/payment of all covered PIP benefits (May 31, 2023). It includes medical treatment, related expenses, lost wages, and administrative processing. It does not cover property damage claims or liability claims against third parties.

## Definitions
*   **PIP (Personal Injury Protection):** A component of auto insurance that covers medical expenses and lost wages for the insured and other occupants of the vehicle, regardless of fault.
*   **Medical Lien:** A legal claim placed on personal injury settlements to ensure healthcare providers are paid for services rendered.
*   **UCR (Usual, Customary, and Reasonable):** The process used to determine the appropriate amount to pay for a medical service, based on charges for the same or similar services in the same geographic area and by practitioners of similar specialty.
*   **IME (Independent Medical Examination):** An evaluation by a physician chosen by the insurance company to confirm the extent of injuries and the necessity of ongoing treatment.
*   **WC (Workers' Compensation):** A form of insurance providing wage replacement and medical benefits to employees injured in the course of employment. PIP benefits are typically secondary to WC.

## Case Narrative: Claim ID #PIP-JD-2022-001
**Insured:** Jane Doe
**Policy Number:** XYZ-789-01-A
**Date of Accident:** December 1, 2022, 10:30 AM
**Location:** Intersection of Main St and Elm Ave, Anytown, USA
**Vehicle:** 2020 Honda Civic, VIN: GHFYU789012345678
**Insured's Role:** Driver
**Claim Initiated:** December 2, 2022

Jane Doe was involved in a rear-end collision on December 1, 2022. She reported immediate neck and upper back pain. She was transported by ambulance to Anytown General Hospital Emergency Room.

### Initial Treatment and Diagnosis
*   **Date:** December 1, 2022
*   **Provider:** Anytown General Hospital ER
*   **Diagnosis:** Cervical strain, thoracic contusion.
*   **Treatment:** X-rays of cervical and thoracic spine (negative for fracture), pain medication (ibuprofen 800mg, cyclobenzaprine), discharge with recommendation for follow-up with primary care physician.
*   **ER Bill Amount:** $2,500.00
*   **Ambulance Bill Amount:** $850.00

### Follow-up Care
*   **Primary Care Physician (Dr. Smith):**
    *   **Date:** December 5, 2022
    *   **Diagnosis:** Persistent cervicalgia, myalgia (neck and upper back).
    *   **Treatment Plan:** Prescribed physical therapy (PT) 3 times per week for 6 weeks, refer to orthopedist if no improvement.
    *   **Clinic Visit Bill:** $150.00
*   **Physical Therapy (Rehab Solutions Clinic):**
    *   **Start Date:** December 7, 2022
    *   **Total Sessions:** 18 sessions over 6 weeks.
    *   **Total PT Bills:** $2,700.00 ($150 per session)
*   **Orthopedic Specialist (Dr. Jones):**
    *   **Date:** January 20, 2023 (after 6 weeks of PT with limited improvement)
    *   **Diagnosis:** Chronic cervical strain, trapezius spasm.
    *   **Treatment:** Recommended MRI of cervical spine, continued PT for another 4 weeks, prescription for stronger muscle relaxant (Flexeril).
    *   **Clinic Visit Bill:** $300.00
*   **MRI Scan (Diagnostic Imaging Center):**
    *   **Date:** January 25, 2023
    *   **Findings:** Degenerative changes at C5-C6, but no acute disc herniation or nerve impingement directly attributable to the accident.
    *   **MRI Bill:** $1,200.00

### Lost Wages Claim
Jane Doe is employed as an administrative assistant at ABC Corp. She works 40 hours per week at an hourly rate of $25.00.
*   **Initial Time Off:** 5 days (December 4-8, 2022) immediately following the accident due to pain and difficulty concentrating.
*   **Subsequent Reduced Schedule:** 2 hours reduction per day for 4 weeks (December 11, 2022 - January 5, 2023) due to physical therapy appointments and persistent discomfort.
*   **Total Hours Lost:** (5 days * 8 hours/day) + (4 weeks * 5 days/week * 2 hours/day) = 40 hours + 40 hours = 80 hours.
*   **Total Lost Wages Claimed:** 80 hours * $25.00/hour = $2,000.00

## Requirements/Criteria for PIP Coverage
*   **Policy in Effect:** Policy XYZ-789-01-A was active on December 1, 2022, with PIP limits of $10,000 for medical expenses and $2,500 for lost wages.
*   **Accident Involvement:** Jane Doe was an occupant of the insured vehicle involved in a defined motor vehicle accident.
*   **Medical Necessity:** All medical treatments must be deemed medically necessary for the injuries sustained in the accident.
*   **Wage Loss Proof:** Documentation of employment, usual wages, and verification of time lost from work from the employer.
*   **Timely Notification:** Claim was reported within 24 hours of the accident.

## Decision Logic and Adjudication Process

### Medical Bill Processing
*   **ER and Ambulance Bills:** Processed at 100% of the billed amount, as services were emergent and directly related to the accident.
    *   Anytown General Hospital ER: Paid $2,500.00
    *   Ambulance Service: Paid $850.00
*   **Primary Care Visit:** Processed at 100% of the billed amount.
    *   Dr. Smith: Paid $150.00
*   **Physical Therapy Bills:**
    *   Initial 6 weeks (18 sessions): Approved and paid at 100% of UCR, which matched the billed rate of $150/session. Total $2,700.00.
    *   Subsequent 4 weeks (Dr. Jones' recommendation): **Adjuster Review Required.** Based on the MRI findings showing pre-existing degenerative changes and absence of acute injury, an IME was requested by the insurer.
        *   **IME Decision Trigger:** Lack of significant objective improvement after initial PT course, and MRI findings not directly supporting prolonged, intense therapy for acute injury.
        *   **IME Verdict (Dr. Evans, Orthopedic Specialist):** After reviewing records and examining Jane Doe on February 15, 2023, Dr. Evans concluded that 2 additional weeks of PT were reasonable, but further PT beyond that would likely not yield significant functional improvement directly attributable to the December 1st accident. He recommended a home exercise program.
        *   **Adjudication:** The insurer approved and paid for 6 additional PT sessions ($900.00) based on the IME recommendation, bringing the total paid PT to 24 sessions. The remaining 6 sessions claimed were denied.
*   **Orthopedic Specialist Visit (Dr. Jones):** Processed at 100% of the billed amount.
    *   Dr. Jones: Paid $300.00
*   **MRI Scan Bill:** Processed at 100% of UCR. The billed amount of $1,200.00 was within the UCR guidelines for the geographic area.
    *   Diagnostic Imaging Center: Paid $1,200.00

### Lost Wages Processing
*   **Documentation Required:**
    *   Employer's Statement of Lost Wages (signed and dated by ABC Corp HR).
    *   Pay stubs for 3 months prior to the accident.
    *   Doctor's note (Attending Physician's Statement - APS) explicitly stating the period Jane Doe was unable to work or required reduced hours due to accident-related injuries.
*   **Evidence Provided:** All required documentation was submitted. Dr. Smithâ€™s initial APS noted an inability to work for 5 days and recommended reduced activity/hours for 4 weeks for PT.
*   **Calculation:**
    *   Initial 40 hours: Approved, as supported by APS.
    *   Reduced schedule 40 hours: Approved, as supported by APS.
*   **Adjudication:** Total lost wages of $2,000.00 were approved and paid. This amount was within the policy's lost wage limit of $2,500.

## Exceptions
*   No significant exceptions were noted in this case. The IME decision regarding the extent of physical therapy was a standard process for disputes over medical necessity.
*   There was no indication of Workers' Compensation applicability, as the accident occurred outside of work hours and duties.
