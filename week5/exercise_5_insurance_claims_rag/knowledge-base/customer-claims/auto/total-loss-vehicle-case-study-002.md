# Auto Total Loss Vehicle Case Study 002

## Overview
This document details Case Study 002 for an auto insurance total loss claim. It provides a comprehensive record of the claim process from initial notification to final settlement, including vehicle valuation, salvage operations, and indemnification determination. The purpose is to serve as a reference for agents handling similar total loss scenarios, demonstrating a typical workflow and decision-making process.

## Scope
This case study applies to claims involving passenger vehicles declared a total loss due to collision damage where the cost of repair exceeds the Actual Cash Value (ACV) or a predefined percentage of ACV. It covers standard valuation techniques, salvage procedures, and payout calculations for insured vehicles. Excluded are claims involving theft, fire-only losses, or commercial vehicles.

## Definitions
*   **Actual Cash Value (ACV):** The fair market value of the vehicle immediately before the loss, considering factors like age, mileage, condition, and options.
*   **Total Loss Threshold:** The point at which a vehicle is deemed uneconomical to repair. For State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company, this is typically when repair costs reach or exceed 75% of the ACV.
*   **Salvage Value:** The amount that can be recovered from selling the damaged vehicle for parts or scrap.
*   **Indemnification:** The principle of restoring the insured to their financial position prior to the loss, without profiting from the loss.
*   **CCC Information Services (CCC One):** A commonly used third-party valuation service providing market-driven ACV reports.

## Vehicle Valuation

### Valuation Methodology
Adjuster Vance utilized CCC Information Services (CCC One) to determine the Actual Cash Value (ACV) of the 2018 Honda Civic EX. This method compares the insured vehicle to similar vehicles recently sold in the local market.

### Valuation Report Details
**Date of Valuation:** November 1, 2023
**Valuation Source:** CCC Information Services Report ID: SF-2023-11-002
**Key Comparables Used:**
    *   **Comparable 1:** 2018 Honda Civic EX, 62,500 miles, similar options, sold 10/25/2023 for $21,000 (Local Dealer: Auto Emporium)
    *   **Comparable 2:** 2018 Honda Civic EX, 68,000 miles, similar options, sold 10/20/2023 for $20,500 (Private Sale: Online Classifieds)
    *   **Comparable 3:** 2018 Honda Civic EX, 65,000 miles, slightly higher mileage, sold 10/22/2023 for $20,800 (Local Dealer: CarZone)

**Adjustments for Insured Vehicle (Mr. Jenkins' 2018 Honda Civic EX):**
*   **Mileage:** 63,000 miles (average of comparables: 65,166 miles) â€“ No significant adjustment.
*   **Condition:** Rated "Good" prior to loss based on photos and policyholder statement (minor scratches consistent with age, no major dents). Comparables ranged from "Good" to "Very Good."
*   **Options:** Standard EX trim package, no aftermarket modifications.
*   **Pre-Loss photos:** Policyholder provided photos from 6 months prior, showing well-maintained condition.

**Calculated Actual Cash Value (ACV):**
*   Base ACV (from CCC One): $20,750.00
*   Sales Tax (6.0% for California): $1,245.00
*   License & Title Fees: $75.00
*   **Total ACV:** **$22,070.00**

### Initial Repair Estimate
**Date of Estimate:** October 31, 2023
**Estimating Source:** Springfield Auto Body & Towing (using CCC One Estimating Software)
**Estimated Repair Cost:** $17,500.00
**Total Loss Threshold Check:** $17,500.00 (Repair Cost) / $20,750.00 (Base ACV) = 84.34%

## Total Loss Determination

**Decision Date:** November 2, 2023
**Decision Maker:** Adjuster Michael Vance
**Decision Logic:** Since the estimated repair cost of $17,500.00 exceeds the company's total loss threshold of 75% of the vehicle's ACV ($20,750.00 * 0.75 = $15,562.50), the vehicle was declared a total loss.

## Exceptions and Special Considerations

*   **Timeliness:** The entire process from FNOL to final payout was completed within 18 days, demonstrating efficient handling.
