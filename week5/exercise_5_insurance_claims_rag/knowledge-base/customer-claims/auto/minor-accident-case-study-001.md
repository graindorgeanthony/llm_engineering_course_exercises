# Auto Minor Accident Case Study 001

## Overview/Purpose
This document details Claim ID: A2023-01-15-001, a specific case involving a minor auto accident. The purpose is to serve as a reference for agents handling similar minor auto claims, illustrating the typical assessment process, repair authorization, and final settlement. It highlights the decision-making criteria used for classifying the claim as minor, authorizing specific repairs, and determining the payout.

## Scope
This case study covers the lifecycle of Claim ID: A2023-01-15-001, from initial incident report to final settlement. It includes details of vehicle damage, policy coverage application, repair estimates, and payout.
Excluded from this scope are claims involving total loss declarations, bodily injury, or complex third-party liability disputes.

## Definitions
*   **Claimant:** The policyholder who filed the claim (Ms. Eleanor Vance).
*   **Insured Vehicle:** The vehicle covered by the policy involved in the accident (2018 Honda Civic).
*   **Third-Party Vehicle:** The other vehicle involved in the collision (2020 Toyota Corolla).
*   **Minor Accident:** An accident resulting in cosmetic or superficial damage, with repair costs typically below 15% of the vehicle's Actual Cash Value (ACV) and no structural damage or reported injuries.
*   **ACV (Actual Cash Value):** The market value of the vehicle immediately before the loss, considering depreciation.
*   **Repair Estimate:** A detailed breakdown of the cost of parts, labor, and materials required to restore a damaged vehicle to its pre-loss condition.
*   **Salvage Value:** The estimated value of a damaged vehicle if it were to be sold for parts or scrapped (not applicable in this minor accident case).

## Claim Narrative

### Incident Description
On January 15, 2023, at approximately 10:30 AM PST, Ms. Eleanor Vance (Policyholder, Policy # AUTO-453789) was reversing her 2018 Honda Civic (VIN: JHAFK2F5XJXXXXXXXXX) from a parking spot at the "Green Valley Shopping Center" (123 Main Street, Anytown, CA 90210). She made contact with a stationary 2020 Toyota Corolla (License Plate: 7XYZ456, owned by Mr. Robert Smith). The impact was low-speed, resulting in minor damage to both vehicles. No injuries were reported by either party. A police report (Case # ATN-2023-005) was filed, assigning fault to Ms. Vance.

### Initial Assessment and Triage
*   **Date of Report:** January 15, 2023, 11:15 AM PST
*   **Agent Assigned:** Sarah Jenkins
*   **Initial Damage Assessment:** Based on photos provided by Ms. Vance, visible damage included a scuff and minor dent on the rear bumper of the Honda Civic, and a paint scratch on the front passenger side fender of the Toyota Corolla.
*   **Claim Classification Decision Logic:** The claim was classified as "Minor Auto Accident" due to:
    1.  No reported bodily injuries.
    2.  Visible damage appeared superficial and cosmetic.
    3.  Reported low-speed impact.
    4.  Both vehicles were drivable from the scene.
    5.  Estimated initial repair costs were anticipated to be well below the total loss threshold (ACV of Honda Civic: $18,000; ACV of Toyota Corolla: $23,000).

## Policy Coverage & Eligibility

### Policy Details (Ms. Eleanor Vance)
*   **Policy Number:** AUTO-453789
*   **Effective Dates:** 2022-07-01 to 2023-06-30
*   **Coverage Type:** Comprehensive and Collision
*   **Collision Deductible:** $500
*   **Liability Coverage:**
    *   Bodily Injury: $100,000 per person / $300,000 per accident
    *   Property Damage: $50,000 per accident

### Eligibility for Coverage
Ms. Vance's policy covers collision damage to her own vehicle, subject to the $500 deductible. It also covers property damage liability to the third-party vehicle up to $50,000, as Ms. Vance was deemed at fault by the police report.

## Settlement

### Calculation for Insured Vehicle (Honda Civic)
*   Approved Repair Cost: $781.10
*   Less Deductible: $500.00
*   **Net Payout to Claimant (Ms. Vance):** $281.10 (Paid to Ms. Vance directly, upon submission of proof of repair/invoice)

### Calculation for Third-Party Vehicle (Toyota Corolla)
*   Approved Repair Cost: $197.95
*   Less Deductible: $0.00 (No deductible applies for third-party property damage)
*   **Net Payout to Third Party (Mr. Smith):** $197.95 (Paid to Quality Bodyworks directly, per Mr. Smith's authorization)

### Settlement Summary
*   **Total Claim Payout:** $281.10 (to Ms. Vance) + $197.95 (to Quality Bodyworks on behalf of Mr. Smith) = **$479.05**
*   **Settlement Date:** January 25, 2023
*   **Claim Status:** Closed on January 25, 2023.

## Learning Points for Future Claims
*   **Prompt Photo Submission:** Ms. Vance's clear initial photos expedited the triage process. Encourage claimants to submit detailed photos immediately.
*   **Consistent Fault Determination:** Police report clear; however, in cases without police reports, ensure diligent investigation for fault determination before authorizing repairs.
*   **Standardized Repair Networks:** Utilizing approved repair shops (AutoPro Certified, Quality Bodyworks) ensures consistent pricing and quality, simplifying authorization.
*   **Deductible Application:** Clear communication of deductible application to the claimant on day one minimizes disputes during settlement.
