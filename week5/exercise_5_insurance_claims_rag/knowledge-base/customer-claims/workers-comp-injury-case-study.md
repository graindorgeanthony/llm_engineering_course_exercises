# Workers' Compensation Injury Claim Case Study: John Doe

## Overview/Purpose

This document presents a detailed case study of a workers' compensation claim filed by John Doe, an employee of Acme Manufacturing Inc., following a workplace injury. The purpose is to illustrate the process of a typical workers' compensation claim, from injury notification and initial medical assessment to treatment, rehabilitation, and return-to-work protocols. This case study will serve as a reference for handling similar claims, highlighting key decision points and documentation requirements.

## Scope

This case study covers the period from the date of injury (January 15, 2023) through the claim closure date (October 20, 2023). It includes details of the injury event, initial reporting, medical evaluations, treatment plans, temporary disability benefits, return-to-work clearances, and final settlement. It applies to claims arising from physical injuries sustained during the course of employment for registered employees.

## Definitions

*   **Date of Injury (DOI):** The date the workplace accident or exposure occurred.
*   **Temporary Total Disability (TTD):** Benefits paid to an injured worker who is temporarily unable to perform any work due to their injury.
*   **Maximum Medical Improvement (MMI):** The point at which an injured worker's condition has stabilized and is not expected to improve further with additional medical treatment.
*   **Return-to-Work (RTW):** The process of an injured employee resuming their employment, potentially with modified duties or accommodations.
*   **Occupational Health Clinic (OHC):** A specialized medical facility that focuses on workplace-related injuries and illnesses.

## Claim Narrative

### Incident Details

*   **Claimant Name:** John Doe
*   **Employee ID:** 78901
*   **Employer:** Acme Manufacturing Inc., Assembly Line Division
*   **Date of Injury (DOI):** January 15, 2023
*   **Time of Injury:** Approximately 10:30 AM PST
*   **Location of Injury:** Assembly Line Station 3, Acme Manufacturing Plant, 123 Industrial Way, Anytown, CA 90210
*   **Injury Type:** Right wrist sprain and potential fracture
*   **Description of Incident:** While operating a pneumatic wrench to tighten bolts on an assembly line product, the wrench unexpectedly recoiled, causing John Doe's right wrist to twist awkwardly against the conveyor belt frame. John immediately reported sharp pain and swelling.
*   **Witnesses:** Jane Smith (Supervisor, Employee ID: 00112), Bob Johnson (Co-worker, Employee ID: 55443)

### Initial Reporting and First Aid

*   **Immediate Action:** John Doe reported the injury to his supervisor, Jane Smith, within 10 minutes of the incident.
*   **First Aid Administered:** Plant first aid personnel applied an ice pack and immobilized the wrist with a basic splint.
*   **Incident Report:** Acme Manufacturing Incident Report #2023-01-15-001 completed by Jane Smith on January 15, 2023, 11:00 AM PST.

## Medical Treatment Phase

### Emergency Care / Initial Diagnosis

*   **Date of First Medical Visit:** January 15, 2023, 11:45 AM PST
*   **Facility:** Anytown Urgent Care Center
*   **Diagnosis:** Right wrist sprain (ICD-10 Code: S63.501A). X-rays were inconclusive for fracture but suggested soft tissue damage.
*   **Treatment Plan:** RICE (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation), over-the-counter pain relievers, and a referral to the Acme Manufacturing designated Occupational Health Clinic (OHC) for further evaluation within 48 hours.
*   **Work Status:** Out of work, temporary total disability (TTD) recommended for 3 days.

### Follow-up Medical Care and Specialist Involvement

*   **Date of OHC Visit:** January 17, 2023, 9:00 AM PST
*   **Clinic:** Anytown Occupational Health Clinic
*   **Physician:** Dr. Elizabeth Chen, Orthopedic Specialist
*   **Further Diagnostics:** MRI of the right wrist performed on January 18, 2023. MRI results confirmed a Grade II ligamentous sprain of the scapholunate ligament and tendinopathy of the extensor carpi ulnaris (ECU) tendon. No fracture identified.
*   **Revised Treatment Plan:**
    *   Immobilization with a volar wrist splint for 4 weeks.
    *   Prescription anti-inflammatory medication (Ibuprofen 800mg, three times daily).
    *   Physical Therapy (PT) referral, 3 times per week for 6 weeks, beginning February 14, 2023.
    *   Follow-up with Dr. Chen on February 13, 2023, and April 3, 2023.
*   **Work Status Recommendation:** Continued TTD for 4 weeks, then re-evaluation for modified duty.

### Rehabilitation and Physical Therapy

*   **PT Start Date:** February 14, 2023
*   **PT Facility:** Anytown Physical Therapy
*   **PT Goals:** Improve wrist range of motion, strengthen forearm and wrist muscles, reduce pain, restore functional use of the hand and wrist.
*   **Progress:** John Doe demonstrated good compliance with PT, showing gradual improvement in strength and flexibility. By week 4 of PT, he could perform light gripping and wrist movements with minimal pain.

## Disability and Benefits Management

### Temporary Disability Payments

*   **Initial TTD Period:** January 15, 2023 - February 12, 2023 (Approved based on initial OHC recommendation).
*   **Extended TTD Period:** February 13, 2023 - March 24, 2023 (Approved based on Dr. Chen's note of March 10, 2023, indicating continued inability to perform pre-injury duties).
*   **Payment Calculation:** 66.67% of John Doe's average weekly wage ($900), resulting in a weekly TTD payment of $600. Payments were issued bi-weekly.

### Medical Bill Processing

*   All medical bills from Anytown Urgent Care Center, Anytown Occupational Health Clinic, and Anytown Physical Therapy were submitted directly to the workers' compensation carrier (InnovateSure Insurance).
*   **Approval Process:** Bills were reviewed and approved within 7-10 business days of submission, adhering to state fee schedules. All approved charges were paid directly to providers.

## Return-to-Work (RTW) Protocols

### Modified Duty Evaluation

*   **Date of Evaluation:** March 20, 2023 (with Dr. Chen)
*   **MMI Status:** Not yet at MMI.
*   **Work Restrictions (March 20, 2023):**
    *   No lifting over 5 lbs with the right hand.
    *   No repetitive gripping, pushing, or pulling with the right hand.
    *   Limited keyboard use (1 hour total per day).
    *   No work above shoulder height.
    *   Must be able to take frequent breaks (every 60 minutes for 5-10 minutes).
*   **Acme Manufacturing Modified Duty Offer:** On March 25, 2023, Acme Manufacturing offered John Doe a modified duty position in the administrative department, performing data entry (modified to adhere to keyboard restrictions) and filing with left-hand dominance.
*   **Acceptance:** John Doe accepted the modified duty position, commencing March 27, 2023.
*   **Wage Impact:** John Doe returned to work at his full pre-injury wage, as per Acme Manufacturing company policy for modified duty. TTD benefits ceased on March 26, 2023.

### Full Duty Release and MMI

*   **Date of Final Medical Evaluation:** October 1, 2023 (with Dr. Chen)
*   **MMI Status:** John Doe reached Maximum Medical Improvement (MMI).
*   **Final Work Status:** Full duty with no restrictions, effective October 2, 2023. Dr. Chen noted excellent recovery and no permanent impairment.
*   **Physical Exam Findings (Oct 1, 2023):** Full range of motion in the right wrist, 5/5 strength in all muscle groups, patient reports no pain with activities of daily living or simulation of previous work tasks.

## Claim Closure

*   **Date of Claim Closure:** October 20, 2023
*   **Reason:** Claimant reached MMI, returned to full duty without restrictions, and all medical bills and temporary disability periods were resolved. No permanent disability rating was assigned.
*   **Claim Costs Summary:**
    *   Medical Expenses (Urgent Care, OHC, MRI, PT, Medications): $7,850.00
    *   Temporary Total Disability Payments: $7,200.00 (12 weeks x $600/week)
    *   Administrative/Processing Fees (Carrier): $N/A (covered by premium)
    *   Total Settled Amount: $15,050.00 (Excluding carrier overhead)

## Exceptions

*   No exceptions were noted in this specific case. All parties adhered to established procedures and timelines.
*   **Hypothetical Exception:** Had John Doe been diagnosed with a permanent impairment, a Permanent Partial Disability (PPD) rating would have been obtained, and additional compensation might have been negotiated or adjudicated.

## Documentation Requirements

The following documentation was critical for the successful processing and resolution of this claim:

*   Employee's First Report of Injury (Acme Manufacturing Form).
*   Supervisor's Incident Report (Acme Manufacturing Form #2023-01-15-001).
*   Worker's Compensation Claim Form (State-specific form, e.g., DWC-1 in California).
*   All medical reports from Anytown Urgent Care, Anytown Occupational Health Clinic (Dr. Chen), and Anytown Physical Therapy.
*   MRI reports and images.
*   Work Status Reports (Doctor's Notes/Restrictions) from Dr. Chen at each visit.
*   Wage statements/pay stubs for calculation of average weekly wage.
*   Acknowledgement of Return to Modified Duty form (Acme Manufacturing HR).
*   Acknowledgement of Return to Full Duty form (Acme Manufacturing HR).
*   All correspondence between the claimant, employer, medical providers, and workers' compensation carrier.

## Timelines

*   **Injury Report to Employer:** Within 24 hours (John Doe reported instantly).
*   **Employer to Carrier Notification:** Within 3 days of knowledge of injury (Acme Manufacturing notified InnovateSure Insurance on January 16, 2023).
*   **Carrier Decision on Compensability:** Within 14 days of receiving claim (InnovateSure accepted claim on January 25, 2023).
*   **First TTD Payment:** Within 14 days of being out of work, if claim accepted (John Doe received first TTD payment February 1, 2023).
*   **Medical Bill Payment:** Within 45 days of receipt (InnovateSure consistently paid within 10-15 days).
*   **Claim Closure:** Approximately 9 months from DOI.
