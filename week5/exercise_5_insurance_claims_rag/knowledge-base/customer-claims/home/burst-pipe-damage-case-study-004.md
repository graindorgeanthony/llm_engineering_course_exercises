# Home Burst Pipe Damage Case Study 004

## Overview/Purpose
This document details Claim #2023-HOME-004, a home insurance claim filed due to significant water damage caused by a burst pipe. This case study illustrates the typical process from initial report to final repair, including damage assessment, drying protocols, and cost breakdowns, to serve as a reference for handling similar claims efficiently.

## Scope
This case study covers the incident resolution for policyholder Jane M. Doe located at 123 Oak Avenue, Anytown, USA, Policy ID HM-789012. It includes the initial report, emergency services, damage assessment, mitigation efforts (drying), repair estimates, and final settlement. It does not cover legal disputes or subrogation efforts, which were not applicable in this case.

## Definitions
*   **Insured:** Jane M. Doe, policyholder of HM-789012.
*   **Date of Loss (DOL):** The date the incident occurred or was discovered.
*   **Mitigation:** Actions taken immediately after a loss to prevent further damage (e.g., water extraction, drying).
*   **Structural Drying:** The process of removing moisture from building materials (walls, floors, framing) using specialized equipment.
*   **Xactimate:** Industry-standard software used for estimating construction and repair costs for insurance claims.
*   **Actual Cash Value (ACV):** The value of an item at the time of loss, considering depreciation.
*   **Replacement Cost Value (RCV):** The cost to replace an item with a new one of similar kind and quality.

## Claim Narrative

### Incident Report
On September 15, 2023, at approximately 08:30 AM EST, Ms. Jane Doe reported a burst pipe incident in her residence at 123 Oak Avenue, Anytown, USA. She discovered significant water accumulation in the basement and on the main floor after returning from a weekend trip. The burst occurred in a Â¾-inch copper supply line leading to the washing machine in the laundry room on the first floor. Water flowed for an estimated 48-72 hours before discovery.

### Emergency Response and Mitigation
*   **09/15/2023, 09:15 AM:** Emergency plumber (Anytown Plumbing) dispatched. Water main shut off.
*   **09/15/2023, 11:30 AM:** Initial water extraction initiated by RapidDry Restoration Services. Approximately 300 gallons of freestanding water were removed from the basement and first-floor areas.
*   **09/15/2023, 04:00 PM:** RapidDry Restoration Services began placement of drying equipment: 10 commercial air movers, 3 large dehumidifiers (LGR 2000), and 2 heat distribution units in affected areas (basement, laundry room, kitchen, living room).

## Damage Assessment

### Affected Areas
*   **Basement:** Finished basement with drywall, carpet over concrete slab. Water intrusion covered approximately 600 sq ft.
*   **First Floor:** Laundry room (vinyl flooring), kitchen (hardwood flooring), partial living room (hardwood flooring). Water affected approximately 350 sq ft.

### Detailed Damage Analysis
*   **Drywall:** 4 ft flood cut required in basement across 60 linear feet. Sections of drywall in laundry and kitchen require removal due to saturation.
*   **Flooring:**
    *   Basement: Carpet and pad completely saturated, requiring removal and disposal (600 sq ft).
    *   First Floor (Kitchen/Living Room): Hardwood flooring showed significant cupping and buckling over 350 sq ft. Visual inspection and moisture meter readings (average 18-22% MC) indicated non-restorable condition, requiring removal.
    *   First Floor (Laundry): Vinyl flooring over subfloor, subfloor showed moisture intrusion, requiring removal (50 sq ft).
*   **Insulation:** Soffit and wall cavity insulation in basement affected, requiring removal and replacement (R-13 batt, 60 linear ft).
*   **Cabinetry:** Base cabinets in kitchen showed signs of water wicking, requiring removal of base toe kicks and internal drying. Minimal delamination observed, deemed restorable.
*   **Contents:** Extensive damage to personal contents in basement, including soaked books, electronics, and furniture. A detailed contents inventory was initiated.

## Drying Process and Monitoring
*   **09/16/2023 - 09/22/2023:** Monitoring occurred daily. Moisture content readings were taken from structural components (framing, subfloor, drywall) using a Tramex BMR moisture meter. Humidity and temperature readings were recorded.
*   **Target Readings:** Goal for wood framing and subfloor was below 12% MC. Drywall target was below 15% MC and within 3% of unaffected areas.
*   **Results:** By September 22, 2023, all affected structural materials met drying standards. Equipment was removed.

*   Water Extraction & Disposal: $850.00

*   Labor (Setup, Monitoring, Teardown): $1,500.00
*   Waste Disposal (Carpet, Pad, Drywall): $600.00

### Reconstruction Estimate (Precision Builders, Xactimate Estimate #PB-HM-004-2023)
*   **Demolition & Prep:**

    *   Hardwood Flooring Removal (350 sq ft): $1,225.00
    *   Vinyl Flooring & Subfloor Removal (50 sq ft): $200.00
*   **Materials & Installation:**
    *   New Drywall (60 LF): $960.00 (including taping, mudding, texture)
    *   Insulation (R-13 Batt, 60 LF): $300.00
    *   New Carpet & Pad (Basement, 600 sq ft @ $3.50/sq ft RCV): $2,100.00
    *   New Hardwood Flooring (Kitchen/Living, 350 sq ft @ $7.00/sq ft RCV): $2,450.00
    *   New Vinyl Flooring (Laundry, 50 sq ft @ $2.50/sq ft RCV): $125.00
*   **Painting:**
    *   Basement Walls (60 LF x 8ft high): $1,200.00

*   **Total Reconstruction: $12,111.00**

### Contents Inventory and Loss
*   A detailed contents inventory was submitted by the insured. Total ACV for damaged unsalvageable contents was $4,500.00. This includes specific items such as a 55-inch smart TV ($800 ACV), various books ($300 ACV), and a modular sofa set ($1,500 ACV). RCV for these items is $6,200.00.

## Decision Logic
*   **Eligibility:** Claim deemed eligible under policy HM-789012 for sudden and accidental water discharge from a plumbing system.
*   **Coverage:** Standard homeowner's policy covers resulting damage to dwelling and contents, subject to deductible.
*   **Deductible:** Policy includes a $1,000 "All Other Perils" deductible.
*   **Settlement Basis:** Dwelling damage settled at RCV unless explicitly stated otherwise. Contents settled at ACV with provision for RCV once replacements are proven.
*   **Mitigation Approval:** Emergency mitigation services approved immediately upon confirmation of loss to prevent further damage, aligning with policy obligations for insured to mitigate losses.
